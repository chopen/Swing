# Swinger Analysis: Feb 16 – Mar 22, 2026

## Overview

Analysis of momentum swing impact across **5 weeks** of NCAA Division I men's basketball, covering approximately **1,700+ games**. Players are ranked by average magnitude-weighted swing impact per game — a metric that measures how much a player contributes to momentum inflection points, scaled by the size of each swing.

## Key Findings

1. **Layden Blocker is the standout swinger of this period.** Elite impact across 3 consecutive weeks with clutch performance in every one — no other player comes close to that consistency.

2. **Big East is the deepest conference for swingers.** Three teams (Marquette, DePaul, Providence) carry Elite or Excellent swingers, and Creighton adds another Excellent performer.

3. **Consistency is extremely rare.** Of hundreds of conference top-3 swingers, only 3 maintained Above Average+ across multiple weeks. Most Elite performances are single-week spikes.

4. **Mid-major programs punch above their weight.** NJIT (412.2), Indiana State (dual Elite), VMI, and FIU all produced swinger depth comparable to or exceeding major conference teams.

5. **Clutch swingers correlate with winning.** Players with the CLUTCH badge (final 5 minutes, score within 12) appear disproportionately among the highest-impact swingers, suggesting that the best momentum shifters perform under pressure.

## Methodology

- **Weighted Impact**: Each player's possession score during a momentum inflection is multiplied by the swing's magnitude. Bigger swings carry more weight.
- **Inflection Detection**: Second derivative of the momentum curve identifies where momentum changes direction.
- **Clutch**: Final 5 minutes of regulation with score differential within 12 points.
- **Data Source**: ESPN play-by-play data for all NCAA Division I CBB games, Feb 16 – Mar 22, 2026.
- **Ranking**: Players ranked by average weighted impact per game within each Monday–Sunday week, top 3 per conference.
