## What H2H Reveals About MVIX/MRVI Limitations

1. **Calm ≠ Good.** Nebraska has the lowest MVIX among mid-tier seeds but keeps losing. Low volatility can mean a team that doesn't generate enough momentum to beat elite opponents.

2. **Late-season form matters.** UNC and Michigan both have strong MVIX/MRVI profiles built over 10+ games, but their most recent results show declining performance. The rolling window smooths out recent drops.

3. **Conference familiarity not captured.** Teams that play each other twice know each other's tendencies. MVIX/MRVI doesn't account for opponent-specific adjustments.

4. **65% agreement is meaningful but not sufficient.** Roughly 1 in 3 of our picks goes against the H2H evidence. For a production bracket tool, H2H results should be a supplementary input alongside MVIX/MRVI.
